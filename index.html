About

I am a microbial geneticist and third-year Doctoral Fellow in the Slauch Lab at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, advised by James M. Slauch, PhD. My work sits at the intersection of bacterial pathogenesis, post-translational regulation, and antimicrobial biology.

Before joining UIUC, I completed an M.S. in Microbiology at Illinois State University, where I investigated novel antimicrobial compounds and developed bioassays to study the clinical dynamics of antibiotic misuse. I received my B.Sc. in Medical Laboratory Technology from Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology, Ghana.

I am open to collaborations on bacterial genetics, antimicrobial resistance, and virulence regulation. Feel free to reach out at gracious.donkor@gmail.com.

Research

Virulence Regulation in Salmonella Typhimurium

2023 — present

Slauch Lab, Department of Microbiology, UIUC · Advisor: James M. Slauch, PhD

  • Investigating post-transcriptional and post-translational mechanisms governing the Salmonella Pathogenicity Island 1 Type 3 Secretion System (SPI1-T3SS).
  • Characterizing how HilD — the master regulator of SPI1-T3SS — is itself regulated, with a focus on the HilD–HilE protein interaction.
  • Employing high-throughput bacterial genetics, computational analysis, and biochemical approaches to dissect regulatory networks critical to enteric pathogen virulence.

Antimicrobial Resistance & Novel Antimicrobial Development

2020 — 2023

Dahl Lab, Department of Biological Sciences, Illinois State University · Advisor: Jan-Ulrik Dahl, PhD

  • Characterized the mechanism of action of a novel ruthenium–silver-based antimicrobial (AGXX) and its synergy with aminoglycosides against Pseudomonas aeruginosa.
  • Investigated the antimicrobial activity of alkyl pyridinol compounds against Gram-positive pathogens.
  • Developed bioassays to study consequences of bacterial exposure to reactive oxygen and chlorine species.
  • Co-authored an NIH R03 Small Research Grant on antimicrobial adjuvant discovery.
